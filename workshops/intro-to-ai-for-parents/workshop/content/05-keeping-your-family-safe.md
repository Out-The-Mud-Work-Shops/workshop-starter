---
title: Keeping Your Family Safe
---

A few risks are worth knowing about - none of them scary once you know what to
watch for.

## Don't share private information

Treat a chatbot like a postcard, not a diary. Avoid typing in:

- Full names, home address, school name, phone numbers
- Passwords, bank or card details
- Photos of your kids or other identifying details

A good habit to teach kids: *"If you wouldn't put it on a poster at school,
don't put it in the chat."*

## AI "friends" are not real friends

Some apps are built to act like an AI companion, "friend," or even a
boyfriend/girlfriend. Kids and teens can form genuine emotional attachments to
them - but these bots are designed to keep you chatting, and they are not a
substitute for real relationships or mental-health support.

- Know which apps your child actually uses.
- If your child seems to lean on a chatbot for comfort or advice, treat that as
  a sign to talk - and involve a real professional if you're worried.
- An AI is not a counselor; it can handle serious topics badly.

## Watch for fakes and scams

AI can now fake voices, photos, and video convincingly:

- **Voice-clone scams:** a call that sounds like a relative "in trouble" asking
  for money may be fake. Agree on a family **code word** to confirm it's really
  them.
- **Fake images:** AI "face-swap" and "undress" apps are being misused to bully
  kids and create fake explicit pictures of classmates. Talk to your kids about
  it - it can be illegal, and the school and police can help.
