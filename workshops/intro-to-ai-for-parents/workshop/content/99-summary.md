## What you learned

- **What AI is:** a very well-read autocomplete that predicts helpful words -
  great at sounding right, so worth double-checking.
- **How to use it:** open a chat, ask in plain language, and keep the
  conversation going.
- **How to get great answers:** be specific - say who it's for, what you want,
  and how you want it.
- **How to spot trouble:** verify anything that matters, keep private details
  out, and watch for AI "friends," fakes, and scams.
- **How to guide your kids:** AI is a homework *helper*, not a ghost-writer -
  stay curious with them and set the rules together.

## Take it home

Three things to try this week:

1. Use AI for one real task - a meal plan, a tricky email, or explaining
   something to your child.
2. Sit with your kid and have them show you how *they* use it.
3. Put the family AI rules you drafted somewhere everyone can see.
