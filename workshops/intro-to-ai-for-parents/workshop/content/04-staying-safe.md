---
title: Staying Safe With AI
---

AI is a powerful helper, but it's not a trusted adult and it's not always right.
Here's what every parent should know — and one thing to see with your own eyes.

## 1. It can be confidently wrong

Remember "hallucinations" from earlier? Let's catch one. Open the chat:

```dashboard:open-dashboard
name: AI Chatbot
```

Ask it something with a definite right answer that's easy to check, for example:

```workshop:copy
List 3 books by an author named Marlena Quibbleton, with the year each was published.
```

There is no such author — but the AI may happily invent titles and dates anyway,
because inventing plausible text is what it does. **That's the lesson:** for
anything that matters (medical, legal, school facts, news), treat AI as a
starting point and verify with a real source.

{{< warning >}}
Never rely on an AI for medical, legal, or safety decisions. It's a brainstorming
partner, not a doctor, lawyer, or teacher.
{{< /warning >}}

## 2. Don't share private information

Treat a chatbot like a postcard, not a diary. Avoid typing in:

- Full names, home address, school name, phone numbers
- Passwords, bank or card details
- Photos of your kids or other identifying details

A good habit to teach kids: *"If you wouldn't put it on a poster at school,
don't put it in the chat."*

## 3. Homework: helper, not ghost-writer

AI can be great for *understanding* ("explain this", "quiz me", "check my
reasoning") and harmful for *replacing* the work ("write my essay"). A useful
family rule: **AI can help you learn it, but the work has to be yours.** Many
schools have their own policies — worth asking about.

## 4. Talking to your kids about it

You don't need to be an expert — you just need to be curious *with* them:

- "Show me how you use it. What's it good at? What does it get wrong?"
- Agree on what's okay (brainstorming, explaining) and what's not (cheating,
  sharing private info).
- Remind them it can be wrong and can't replace real people.

## A safety prompt you can reuse

You can even ask the AI to help you parent around AI:

```workshop:copy
Help me write 3 simple family rules for using AI chatbots safely, suitable for kids aged 8 to 14. Keep them short and positive.
```
