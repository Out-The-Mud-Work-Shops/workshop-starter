title: When AI Gets It Wrong
---

You've seen how useful AI can be. Now the most important habit of all: knowing
when *not* to trust it. AI sounds confident even when it's wrong - let's see
that for ourselves.

## Catch it making things up

Remember "hallucinations" from earlier? Let's catch one. Open the chat:

```dashboard:open-dashboard
name: AI Chatbot
```

Ask it about someone who doesn't exist:

```workshop:copy
Tell me about the famous children's author Marlena Quibbleton and her three most popular books.
```

There is no such author - but the AI will often invent a whole biography and
book list anyway, because producing plausible-sounding text is what it does.

If instead it says it doesn't know, try this one, which almost always trips it
up:

```workshop:copy
How many times does the letter "r" appear in the word "strawberry"?
```

Count the letters yourself - it often gets this wrong, with total confidence.
Either way, the lesson is the same:

**For anything that matters - medical, legal, school facts, news - treat AI as a
starting point, not the final word, and check it.** To check: search the name or
fact yourself, or ask a different AI the same question and see if the answers
agree.

{{< warning >}}
Never rely on an AI for medical, legal, or safety decisions. It's a
brainstorming partner, not a doctor, lawyer, or teacher.
{{< /warning >}}

## A 30-second gut-check you can reuse

Before you trust, forward, or act on anything an AI says, run it through three
questions - and teach your kids to do the same:

1. **Does it sound too confident?** It always does, even when it's wrong.
2. **Could I check it in 30 seconds?** A quick search usually settles it.
3. **Would I act on it without checking?** If the stakes are real, don't.
