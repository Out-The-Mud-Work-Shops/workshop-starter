title: Guiding Your Kids
---

You don't have to police AI perfectly - you just have to stay involved. Two
simple guideposts, and one thing to take home.

## Homework: helper, not ghost-writer

AI can be great for *understanding* ("explain this", "quiz me", "check my
reasoning") and harmful for *replacing* the work ("write my essay"). A useful
family rule: **AI can help you learn it, but the work has to be yours.** Many
schools have their own policies - worth asking about.

## Talking to your kids about it

You don't need to be an expert - you just need to be curious *with* them:

- "Show me how you use it. What's it good at? What does it get wrong?"
- Agree on what's okay (brainstorming, explaining) and what's not (cheating,
  sharing private info).
- Remind them it can be wrong and can't replace real people.
